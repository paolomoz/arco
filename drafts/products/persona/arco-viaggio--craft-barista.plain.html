Manual pressure. Full control. Pocket-sized.

The Viaggio's lever mechanism lets you control pressure ramp, peak, and decline by feel -- up to 9 bar, entirely in your hands. It's a manual profiling tool that weighs 1.2 kg.

The Viaggio is deceptively capable. Yes, it's a travel espresso maker. But its lever mechanism gives you something most electronic machines don't: true manual pressure profiling. You control the ramp -- slowly build to pre-infusion pressure, hold, then push through to full extraction at up to 9 bar, modulating decline by easing off the lever. With a good hand grinder and a precision scale, you can produce shots that rival machines ten times its size. The fine-grind basket accepts standard espresso-grind coffee (no proprietary pods required), and the stainless steel construction means brew temperature retention is excellent when you preheat with hot water. For the barista who already has a main rig, the Viaggio is the analog companion -- a tool that sharpens your feel for pressure and extraction in a way that electronic machines can't teach.
